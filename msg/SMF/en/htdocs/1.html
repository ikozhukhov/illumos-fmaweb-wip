While a service is disabled, the Solaris Service Manager will not start it. If a running service is disabled (for example, by svcadm disable -t service_fmri), the Service Manager will send a stop command to the service's restarter, which may be the Solaris Service Manager itself. The restarter will stop the service and place it in the disabled state. Temporary disable does not persist past system shutdown.

The Message ID:   SMF-8000-1S indicates that the service is temporarily disabled.

If the Suggested Action fails to start the service, contact Sun Service with the output of svcs -x and svcs -l service_fmri.

See "Predictive Self-Healing" for additional information and reference the "SMF How To Guide"
