If a service is enabled and its dependencies are met, the Solaris Service Manager will send a start command to the service's restarter, which may be the Solaris Service Manager itself. Require-type dependencies are only considered satisfied when the indicated services are running (online or degraded).

The Message ID:   SMF-8000-E2 indicates that the service has a require-type dependency on a service which does not exist. Under such circumstances, the Service Manager will not attempt to start it.

This outcome can indicate a misunderstanding of the requirements of various software components, a misconfiguration of the system, or a defect in Solaris.

If the service remains offline, contact Sun Service with the output of svcs -x and svcs -l service_fmri.

See "Predictive Self-Healing" for additional information.
